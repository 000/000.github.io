This connection to ancient scripture places the amulet within Thailand's rich cultural heritage, making it particularly resonant with devotees who see it as a tangible link to their spiritual and mythological traditions.

Traditional Symbolism in Thai Amulet Metals

Lead in Protective Amulets

Lead has historical use in Pidta amulets, where its density symbolizes the ability to "weigh down" negative energies. The dark color associates with absorption and deflection of harmful forces, enhancing protective qualities [40].

Copper in Sacred Casting

Copper's golden color associates with purity and divine connection. Its conductivity symbolizes spiritual energy channeling, while Venus connections link to harmony and positive relationships, making it versatile for various amulet types.
