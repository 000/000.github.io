Understanding Article 53 and Its Application to Services Procurement

Context of Article 53 within the Treasury Regulation B.E. 2560

The legal analysis focuses on Article 53 of the ระเบียบกระทรวงการคลัง ว่าด้วยการจัดซื้อจัดจ้างและการบริหารพัสดุภาครัฐ พ.ศ. ๒๕๖๐ (Treasury Regulation on Government Procurement and Public Property Management B.E. 2560 (2017)), specifically concerning the procurement of services. Initial research efforts encountered significant difficulty in locating the precise, full text of Article 53 within the Treasury Regulation.

Research Challenge: Early search results provided a PDF link to the Treasury Regulation, with a snippet indicating that "Article 53" pertained to "วิธีการซื้อหรือจ้าง โดยวิธีสอบราคา" (Method of purchase or hire by the method of bidding) [203]. However, subsequent attempts to directly access and extract the full text of Article 53 were unsuccessful.

Interpretation of Article 53: Focus on Bidding Methods for Services

Based on the limited information successfully retrieved, the primary interpretation of Article 53 of the Treasury Regulation B.E. 2560 is that it pertains to the "วิธีการซื้อหรือจ้าง โดยวิธีสอบราคา" (Method of purchase or hire by the method of bidding) [203]. This suggests that Article 53 outlines the procedures and criteria for conducting procurement through a bidding process.

Key Implications for Services Procurement:

  • • Competitive Process: "สอบราคา" (bidding) implies a competitive process where potential service providers submit proposals
  • • Evaluation Criteria: Procuring entities evaluate proposals based on pre-defined criteria, often including price and quality
  • • Service-Specific Considerations: Unique aspects of service procurement require evaluation of qualitative criteria, service provider expertise, and intangible deliverables

Distinction Between Procurement of Supplies and Services

The Public Procurement and Supplies Administration Act B.E. 2560 (2017) delineates specific provisions for different types of procurement, notably distinguishing between the procurement of supplies and the procurement of services. This distinction is crucial for understanding the applicable legal framework.

Article 54 of the Act clarifies that "this Chapter shall apply to the procurement of supplies, but shall not include consulting work and design or construction supervision work" [119]. This exclusion implies that services, particularly those of a consultative, design, or supervisory nature, are governed by different sections or related regulations.

Legal Framework for Challenging Procurement Decisions in Thailand

Overview of the Public Procurement and Supplies Administration Act B.E. 2560

The Public Procurement and Supplies Administration Act B.E. 2560 (2017) serves as the primary legislative framework governing public procurement in Thailand. This Act establishes the rules, procedures, and institutional mechanisms for the acquisition of supplies, services, and construction works by state agencies.

Key Features

  • • Comprehensive complaint and appeal system
  • • Various procurement methods outlined
  • • Qualification criteria for bidders
  • • Procedures for bid submission and evaluation

Institutional Mechanisms

  • • Appeals and Complaints Committee
  • • Transparency and accountability measures
  • • Modernized procurement practices
  • • Anti-corruption provisions

Rights of Bidders to Appeal Procurement Decisions (Section 114)

Section 114 of the Act is pivotal, stipulating that "投标者有权对采购实体违反采购法且使投标者处于不利地位的行为提出上诉" (bidders who have submitted proposals to a state agency for the procurement of supplies have the right to appeal against actions of the procuring entity that violate the procurement law and place the bidder at a disadvantage) [336].

Role and Process of the Appeals Committee

The Public Procurement and Supplies Administration Act B.E. 2560 establishes an Appeals and Complaints Committee (申诉委员会) with significant authority to review and adjudicate procurement disputes.

Committee Composition and Powers:

Leadership

Chaired by the Permanent Secretary of the Ministry of Finance

Membership

Includes ex-officio members from key government agencies and qualified professionals

Decision Timeline

30 days to complete consideration, extendable up to two times (15 working days each)

Powers

Can order re-conduct of procurement or restart from particular stage

Judicial Review and Remedies in Thai Courts

The Act provides a pathway for judicial review in Thai courts for parties dissatisfied with the decisions of the Appeals Committee. If an appellant is not satisfied with the final decision, they have the right to file a lawsuit in court to claim compensation from the state agency [119].

Practical Strategies for Challenging Procurement Decisions under Article 53 for Services

Grounds for Challenging Services Procurement Decisions

While the specific text of Article 53 concerning services procurement was not fully accessible, general grounds for challenging procurement decisions under the overarching Act would likely apply. These typically include violations of the procurement law or regulations by the procuring entity that place a bidder at a disadvantage [336].

Procedural Irregularities

Failure to follow prescribed bidding process, evaluation criteria, or procedural requirements

Unfair Treatment

Inconsistent application of evaluation criteria or bias towards certain bidders

Lack of Transparency

Insufficient information in tender documents or unclear evaluation methods

Qualification Issues

Wrongful disqualification or acceptance of unqualified bidders

Evaluation Errors

Mistakes in scoring or assessment of bids, especially for subjective criteria

Conflict of Interest

Participation of individuals with conflicts compromising fairness

Adherence to Strict Timelines for Filing Complaints and Appeals

Critical Deadline: 7 Working Days

Section 117 of the Act stipulates that an appeal must be filed with the concerned state agency within seven working days from the date the procurement result is announced on the Comptroller-General's Department's information network system [644].

Importance of Thorough Documentation and Evidence Gathering

When challenging a procurement decision, particularly in the context of services procurement, the importance of thorough documentation and meticulous evidence gathering cannot be overstated. The success of a challenge often hinges on the ability to substantiate claims with clear, credible, and relevant evidence.

Essential Documentation Checklist:

  • • Original tender documents and amendments
  • • Bidder's submitted proposal
  • • All communications with procuring entity
  • • Records of bid opening (if applicable)
  • • Public information about evaluation process
  • • Detailed notes of irregularities
  • • Evidence of proposal meeting requirements
  • • Documentation of unfair evaluation

Seeking Legal Counsel and Expert Advice

Navigating the complexities of challenging a procurement decision often necessitates seeking specialized legal counsel and expert advice. Procurement law in Thailand involves an intricate web of statutes, regulations, and administrative procedures.

Legal Counsel Benefits

  • • Guidance on challenge merits and legal framework

  • • Procedural requirement navigation

Expert Consultation

Potential for Injunctive Relief or Other Interim Measures

The potential for obtaining injunctive relief or other interim measures to suspend a procurement process pending the outcome of a challenge is a critical consideration. The Act allows the Appeals Committee to suspend the procurement process if a complaint reveals non-compliance by a state agency, unless a contract has already been signed [119].

General Principles from Thai Procurement Case Law

While specific precedents for Article 53 were not identified, general principles from Thai procurement case law can offer guidance. Thai courts emphasize fundamental principles of transparency, fairness, equal treatment of bidders, and adherence to prescribed procedures.

Supreme Administrative Court Precedent (Judgment No. A. 154/2004)

This case concerning unfair discrimination in a public procurement auction found that the defendant's termination of an auction was unreasonable and constituted unfair discrimination against the plaintiff [375].

Key Principle: Courts are willing to scrutinize procuring entities' actions for fairness and reasonableness, and past practice can be a relevant factor.

Reference to Broader Administrative Law Principles

In the absence of specific precedents, challenges will inevitably draw upon broader administrative law principles. Thai administrative law provides a general framework for reviewing the legality and fairness of actions taken by state agencies.

Legality

Procuring entities must act within powers conferred by law and comply with statutory provisions

Due Process

Bidders entitled to fair hearing and unbiased decision-maker

Reasonableness

Decisions must not be arbitrary, capricious, or grossly disproportionate

Transparency

Process should be open and transparent for public scrutiny

Equal Treatment

All bidders treated equally without discrimination

Legitimate Expectation

Clear representations or consistent practices may create binding expectations
